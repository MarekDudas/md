Bully

Bully, also known as Canis Canem Edit in Europe, is an action-adventure video game developed by Rockstar Vancouver and published by Rockstar Games. It was released in 2006 and gained popularity for its unique setting and gameplay mechanics.

Story

In Bully, players assume the role of Jimmy Hopkins, a troubled teenager who finds himself enrolled in Bullworth Academy, a fictional boarding school. Once you arrive, you realize that the school is divided into fractions: Nerds, Bullies, Preps, Greasers and Jocks and your task is to end up on the top of the foodchain. The game takes place in an open-world environment, allowing players to freely explore the school grounds and the surrounding town.

Game Mechanics

One of the defining aspects of Bully is its unique blend of humor and satire. The game humorously portrays the cliques and stereotypes commonly found in schools, such as jocks, nerds, bullies, and cheerleaders. It tackles issues like bullying, peer pressure, and the struggles of teenage life with a lighthearted and comedic approach. You can also attend classes such as science, shop and PE.
